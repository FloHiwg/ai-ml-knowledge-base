## Contradictions

### AIME listed as both "Saturated" and "Active Frontier"
**Pages involved:** [[evaluation/benchmarks]], [[training/reasoning-models]]  
**Issue:** The two pages give directly conflicting classifications of AIME 2024 as a benchmark.  
**Detail:**
- `evaluation/benchmarks.md` lists AIME under "**Saturated:** GSM-8K (grade school), MATH (high school), AMC, AIME."
- `training/reasoning-models.md` benchmark table lists AIME 2024 as "**Active frontier**" with GPT-4o at 12% vs o3 at ~97%, and uses it as the key demonstration of reasoning model progress.  

**Suggested fix:** Remove AIME from the "Saturated" list in `evaluation/benchmarks.md` and add a note that AIME was *approaching* saturation pre-reasoning models but remains an active frontier in the reasoning era. Or add a qualification: "Saturated for standard LLMs; still active for reasoning models."

## Inconsistent Framing

### DeiT publication year: 2020 vs 2021
**Pages involved:** [[architecture/vision-transformers]], [[summaries/Vision Transformers - by Cameron R. Wolfe, Ph.D.]]  
**Issue:** The wiki cites DeiT as Touvron et al. 2020; the summary cites it as 2021.  
**Detail:**
- `wiki/architecture/vision-transformers.md` (line 44): "DeiT — Data-efficient Image Transformers (Touvron et al. **2020**)"
- `summaries/Vision Transformers.md`: "[Touvron et al., **2021**]"

The arxiv preprint appeared in December 2020; ICML publication was 2021.  

**Suggested fix:** Standardize to 2021 (the peer-reviewed publication year) throughout, as the summary does.

## Missing Cross-Links

### [[training/reasoning-models]] ↔ [[training/continual-learning]]
**Why it matters:** RL's resistance to catastrophic forgetting (RL's Razor, on-policy sampling) is directly explained by the same on-policy property that makes RLVR work in reasoning models. A reader learning about reasoning models should know that the same RL properties also solve continual learning. Currently reasoning-models.md doesn't link to continual-learning.md.

### [[evaluation/benchmarks]] ↔ [[training/reasoning-models]]
**Why it matters:** The benchmarks page discusses saturation (BBH, math benchmarks) without explaining *why* saturation is accelerating. Reasoning models are the proximate cause — linking to reasoning-models.md would give the reader the full picture. Currently the link is only one-way (reasoning-models → benchmarks, not the reverse).

### [[concepts/agi-and-intelligence]] ↔ [[applications/agentic-patterns]]
**Why it matters:** agi-and-intelligence discusses LLM culture and self-play as major unclaimed capability unlocks, which are fundamentally multi-agent concepts. agentic-patterns.md covers multi-agent collaboration patterns without noting that LLM culture/self-play (the most ambitious form) doesn't exist yet. Neither page links to the other.

## Gaps

### PEFT / LoRA
**Mentioned in:** [[training/fine-tuning]], [[training/continual-learning]], [[concepts/agi-and-intelligence]], [[summaries/Understanding and Using SFT]]  
**Issue:** LoRA / PEFT appears in 4 different pages with no dedicated wiki entry. The mentions are consistent but thin — fine-tuning.md has the most coverage ("freeze most weights; only fine-tune a small subset via LoRA") but doesn't explain how LoRA works mechanically (low-rank decomposition, rank hyperparameter, which layers to adapt). A reader following links finds no destination.  
**Suggested fix:** Create `wiki/training/peft-and-lora.md` covering the low-rank decomposition approach, rank hyperparameter, typical targets (attention projection matrices), and the forgetting-prevention motivation.

### Distillation (three distinct uses, no consolidation)
**Mentioned in:** [[training/reasoning-models]], [[architecture/vision-transformers]], [[training/continual-learning]], [[summaries/Demystifying Reasoning Models]]  
**Issue:** "Distillation" is used in three different, non-overlapping senses across pages — knowledge distillation for continual learning (KD loss penalising drift from a teacher), DeiT's distillation token (CNN teacher → ViT student), and DeepSeek-R1's capability distillation (large model SFT dataset → small model). No page connects these or explains that they are different things sharing a name.  
**Suggested fix:** Add a brief disambiguating note the first time each page uses the term, or create a `wiki/concepts/distillation.md` page that distinguishes the three uses and cross-links.
